Questions we get

The honest answers.

Is my financial data secure?

Plaid handles the bank connection the same way Venmo, Robinhood, and every major fintech does — we never see or store your credentials. Gmail and Drive are read-only and scoped to invoices from known vendors or messages mentioning your property address. Nothing else.

Do I need a CPA to use this?

No, but it's more valuable if you have one. Painkiller produces a clean flat-file + PDF export at tax time that most CPAs can drop straight into ProConnect or Lacerte. If you don't have a CPA, the export works for TurboTax Premier too.

What if my house is in an LLC or trust?

Entity tagging lands in v1 on the Portfolio tier — you can attach each property to its owning entity, and exports are grouped accordingly. Multi-entity users should start on Portfolio.

How is my property value estimated?

We blend county assessor records, MLS sales within a half-mile over the last sixty days, and your actual tracked improvements. It's the same methodology appraisers use — not a Zestimate. You can see every comp that feeds the number.

What if I have more than three properties?

The Portfolio tier caps at three properties in v1. If you have more, email us — we're building a family-office tier and we're looking for design partners.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes, monthly with no commitment. Your data export stays yours. Founding member rates only survive as long as your subscription is active — if you cancel and rejoin, you rejoin at current pricing.

You asked: Cheapest way to close my gap?
The cheapest path is usually raising Coverage A on your existing policy — Chubb will quote a new premium without re-underwriting. Based on similar homes in 90210, going from $1.6M to $2.1M typically adds $280–420/yr. That's far cheaper than:
  • Switching carriers ($800–1,500 in re-quote and inspection costs, plus potential coverage gaps during transition)
  • Adding a separate excess dwelling policy ($600–900/yr, more paperwork)
One catch: if you raise Coverage A, your contents (Coverage C) usually moves with it as a percentage. That's actually good — your contents are likely under-insured by a similar amount.
